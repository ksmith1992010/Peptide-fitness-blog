MOTS-c is discussed in metabolic and mitochondrial research circles. Online, it quickly becomes a “body recomp cheat code.” Slow down.

## What’s actually going on

- It’s studied as a mitochondrial-derived peptide with metabolic signaling interest
- Human evidence for consumer wellness claims is not the same as a TikTok montage
- Quality and intended-use context still matter if you see it in research catalogs

## How to read coverage

Prefer reviews and primary papers over supplement-brand blogs. Check whether a claim is mechanistic, animal, or human — then match our [evidence grades](/evidence).
