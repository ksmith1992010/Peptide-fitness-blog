Buying “research materials” online is where a lot of people get wrecked — not always by the molecule, but by **missing paperwork**, swapped lots, and vibes-based purity claims.

This is a literacy checklist. Not a vendor endorsement list.

## What “good” looks like

1. **Clear intended-use language** — RUO / research framing when that’s the lane; no fake pharmacy cosplay.
2. **Lot-linked documentation** — COA that matches the vial in your hand.
3. **Method language** — HPLC / identity methods stated, not just “99% pure” marketing.
4. **Traceable SKU + batch** — you can archive what you received.
5. **Honest “no COA yet” status** — better than a recycled PDF.

## Red flags

- Generic certificates reused across lots
- Miracle human outcomes in the product title
- No lot number on the vial
- Pressure tactics (“last vial”, crypto-only mystery shops with zero docs)
- Sellers who refuse to explain documentation gaps

## A practical order of operations

1. Decide what question you’re actually researching.
2. Read primary literature (PubMed / trials) before catalogs.
3. Vet documentation standards.
4. Only then look at procurement options.

For COA mechanics, read [How to read a COA](/guides/how-to-read-a-coa). For label boundaries, read [Why RUO labels exist](/guides/why-ruo-labels-exist).
