Reconstitution is just dissolving a lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder into a liquid so you can measure a volume.

## Core formulas

- `concentration (mg/mL) = vial mg ÷ water mL`
- `volume (mL) = dose mg ÷ concentration`
- `U-100 units = mL × 100`

Example: 5 mg vial, 2 mL bac water → **2.5 mg/mL**. A 250 mcg (0.25 mg) amount → **0.1 mL** → **10 units** on a U-100 syringe.

## Important reality check

Changing water volume changes concentration and draw size. It does **not** change how much peptide is in the vial.
