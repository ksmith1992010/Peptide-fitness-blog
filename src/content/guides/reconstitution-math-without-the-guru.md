**Peptide reconstitution** is dissolving a lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder into a liquid so you can measure a **volume**. The math is unit conversion — not a stack protocol.

## Core formulas

- `concentration (mg/mL) = vial mg ÷ water mL`
- `volume (mL) = amount mg ÷ concentration`
- `U-100 units = mL × 100`

Example: 5 mg vial + 2 mL bacteriostatic water → **2.5 mg/mL**. A 250 mcg (0.25 mg) amount → **0.1 mL** → **10 units** on a U-100 syringe.

## Reality checks

- Changing water volume changes concentration and draw size. It does **not** change how much peptide is in the vial.
- Calculator outputs are educational. They are not dosing advice for humans.
- Identity / purity still depend on lot docs — see [How to read a peptide COA](/guides/how-to-read-a-coa).
