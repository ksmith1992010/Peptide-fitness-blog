The desk’s peptide-hormones feed regularly surfaces signaling and receptor papers — natriuretic peptides, transmembrane receptor activation, POMC processing fragments, and related physiology.

That is **mechanistic literacy**, not a menu.

## What we take from these papers

- Reminder that “peptide” covers hormones, ligands, and fragments — not one underground category
- Practice spotting **receptor + pathway + model organism** before any outcome claim
- Humility: complex cardiopulm / keratinocyte / CNS pathways do not collapse into Telegram dosing cards

## What we refuse to invent

- Human protocols from knockout / rodent / cell papers
- Compound hype by association (“it’s a peptide hormone paper so my research vial is validated”)
- Clinical advice about blood pressure, pain, skin, or endocrine disease
