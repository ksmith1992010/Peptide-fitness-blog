Science has a ladder. Hype has an elevator that skips floors.

## The ladder

1. **Mechanism / cell work** — can suggest *how* something might work.
2. **Animal studies** — can show signals in a living system that isn’t you.
3. **Early human studies** — safety, dosing exploration, small samples.
4. **Robust clinical evidence** — replicated human outcomes for a defined indication.
5. **Approved/labeled use** — regulators reviewed a package for a specific claim.

## Bro-science failure mode

Someone reads “increased tendon healing in rats,” captions it “injury hack,” and sells a protocol. That’s not evidence literacy. That’s fan fiction with citations.
