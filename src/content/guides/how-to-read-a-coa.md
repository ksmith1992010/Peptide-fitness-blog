A **peptide Certificate of Analysis (COA)** is a lab report for a **specific lot**. It is useful only when it maps to the vial in your hand — not when it’s a recycled marketing PDF.

Amino Brief does not sell peptides. This is documentation literacy for researchers and skeptical buyers of *accessories* / paperwork habits — not a vendor endorsement.

## Definition (pull-quote for citations)

A COA answers three questions for a named batch: **identity** (is this the stated compound?), **purity** (how much of the signal is that compound by the stated method?), and **traceability** (which lab, which lot, which date?).

## Checklist — read in this order

1. **Compound identity** — full name / sequence language if provided; not just a slang SKU.
2. **Lot / batch number** — must match the label on the vial. No match → COA is theater.
3. **Testing lab** — named lab. Self-issued “in-house 99%” with no third party is a weaker signal.
4. **Methods** — HPLC (purity) and preferably MS / identity confirmation. “Tested for purity” with no method is a red flag.
5. **Purity % + chromatogram** — a number without a chromatogram / method conditions is easier to fake.
6. **Test date** — stale reports may not reflect the bottle you received; archive date + lot together.
7. **Analyst / sign-off** — someone accountable signed or issued the report.

Print or PDF-archive the COA next to the lot number. If a source cannot produce lot-linked docs, treat the material as undocumented.

## Classic ways people get played

- Generic COA reused across lots
- Purity number with no chromatogram or method
- Lot on PDF ≠ lot on vial
- “99% pure” social graphics that aren’t lab reports
- QR codes that resolve to a marketing page, not the batch PDF

## What HPLC purity is not

High HPLC purity does **not** prove:

- clinical safety or efficacy
- correct human dosing
- that TikTok’s claim is true
- that the vial was stored or shipped intact after testing

It supports **identity/purity claims for that tested lot** under the stated method — full stop. See [evidence tiers](/guides/evidence-tiers-cell-to-human).
