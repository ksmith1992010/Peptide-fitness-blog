GLP-1 drugs are real medicines with real labels — and a content industry that treats every secondary headline like a new protocol drop.

Amino Brief’s desk keyword-filters ScienceDaily obesity / diabetes / pharmacology feeds for peptide / GLP-1 language. Those blurbs are **signals to investigate**, not conclusions.

## Separate three layers

1. **Approved product + labeled indication** — FDA-context when you are talking about prescription products.
2. **Human trial / observational findings** — need the actual paper or trial ID, not the blog paraphrase.
3. **Influencer remix** — “natural Ozempic,” poison-control scare clips, aging hacks — usually literacy hazards.

## Headline patterns that deserve a slowdown

- **“Natural Ozempic”** — marketing metaphor until you see a molecule, dose form, and regulated pathway.
- **“Works better for some people”** — almost always true for pharmacology; ask *why*, *in whom*, and *with what endpoints*.
- **“Slows aging”** — endpoint discipline required; aging proxies ≠ immortality press releases.
- **Nutrition risk / under-eating stories** — often lifestyle + drug context; still not a DIY playbook from a blog.
