Some desk items are **imaging agents** — peptides designed to bind a target (here, LRRC15-related cancer-associated fibroblast biology) so PET can see it.

That is hospital / translational research language. It is the opposite of “research chemical for your gym bag.”

## Why we publish a literacy note anyway

Because the underground internet flattens every “peptide” headline into the same meme. Naming the lane — **diagnostic / imaging research** — is a safety feature.

## Reader checklist

1. Is the endpoint **image signal**, not performance?
2. Is the setting **oncology / translational**, not sports recovery?
3. Does any catalog listing invent human wellness claims from an imaging paper? (Red flag.)
