**Research Use Only (RUO)** language means the product is positioned for laboratory / research contexts — **not** as an approved medicine for the wellness claims flooding social media.

## Plain definition

RUO is a **labeling / intended-use boundary**. It tells procurement and readers: this SKU is not being sold as a finished drug for treating people under an approved indication.

## What RUO is trying to say

- Not evaluated/approved as a medicine for the marketed human wellness claims you saw online
- Not a substitute for clinical care
- Documentation and sales framing sit in research procurement — not pharmacy counseling

## What RUO is not

- Not a cool “pro tip” that regulators “can’t see”
- Not proof the compound is safe
- Not proof the compound is effective
- Not the same thing as an FDA-approved drug label

## Why journalists and educators cite this

Gray-market peptide coverage often collapses “research chemical,” “compounded medicine,” and “approved drug” into one meme. RUO literacy keeps those lanes separate. Pair with [Approved vs research compounds](/guides/approved-vs-research-compounds) and [Evidence grades](/evidence).

## Awareness take

If your entire information diet about a compound is transformation selfies and Discord screenshots, the RUO label is the adult in the room. Listen to it — then read primary sources, not catalogs.
