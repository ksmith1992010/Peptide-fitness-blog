**Research Use Only (RUO)** language exists because a product is being positioned for laboratory research contexts — not as an approved drug for treating people.

## What RUO is trying to say

- Not evaluated/approved as a medicine for the marketed wellness claims you saw online
- Not a substitute for clinical care
- Documentation and intended use are framed around research procurement

## What RUO is not

- Not a cool “pro tip” that the FDA can’t see
- Not proof the compound is safe
- Not proof the compound is effective

## Awareness take

If your entire information diet about a compound comes from transformation selfies and Discord screenshots, the RUO label is the adult in the room. Listen to it.

For health decisions, talk to a clinician. For literacy, keep reading primary sources.
