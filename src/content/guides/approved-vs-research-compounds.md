Internet discourse loves collapsing everything into one bucket: “peptides.” Don’t.

## Bucket A — Approved peptide medicines

These went through regulated clinical development for specific indications. They have prescribing information, manufacturing controls, and known risk language. Examples people recognize: insulin, certain GLP-1 medicines, and other labeled peptide drugs.

That doesn’t make them risk-free. It means the claim is tied to a regulated dossier.

## Bucket B — Research / experimental compounds

These show up in catalogs, forums, and clinic menus with big promises and thin human data. Many are sold with **research use only** framing. Animal papers get screenshotted like they’re phase 3 trials. They’re not.

## The tell

| Signal | Usually means |
|---|---|
| Prescribing label / NDC / pharmacy pathway | Regulated medicine context |
| “For research only,” no clinical indication | Experimental / RUO framing |
| COA optional or mysterious | Quality theater risk |
| Dose talk from Discord | Anecdote, not evidence |

## Bottom line

Use the same skepticism you’d use for a new pre-workout… then 10x it, because injectable research compounds aren’t candy.

Cross-check names on [ClinicalTrials.gov](https://clinicaltrials.gov/) and PubMed before you let a podcast rewrite your biology syllabus.
