A lot of “didn’t work” stories start with **abuse of the vial**, not the molecule. Storage literacy won’t make research materials into medicines — it will stop you from inventing noise.

## Before reconstitution

- Keep lyophilized material away from heat and light per supplier guidance
- Don’t assume every powder loves the same fridge door habit
- Log lot numbers when you receive them — storage mistakes are hard to debug later

## After reconstitution

- Bacteriostatic water workflows still need clean technique and labeled dates
- Repeated freeze–thaw cycles are a common way to trash a sample’s usefulness for research logging
- “I left it in the gym bag” is not a storage protocol
