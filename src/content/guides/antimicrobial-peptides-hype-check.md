Desk pulls sometimes surface **antimicrobial peptide (AMP)** design and optimization papers. Cool science. Terrible gym folklore fuel.

## What AMPs are (literacy version)

Short peptides studied for activity against microbes. Academic and industrial research lanes exist. That does **not** mean a random vial labeled “AMP blend” is a medicine, a skin miracle, or safe DIY biology.

## Where people get wrecked

- Treating a computational / optimization paper as a product brief
- Confusing **research chemical catalogs** with antimicrobial stewardship
- Skipping contamination / identity / lot documentation (see [How to read a COA](/guides/how-to-read-a-coa))
- Inventing human use stories from in-vitro kill curves

## Evidence posture on Amino Brief

Until a specific AMP product has clear regulatory context and human evidence, our badge stays **literacy / preclinical framing** — not “human proven.” Animal or cell data stays labeled as such.
