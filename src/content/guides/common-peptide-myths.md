## Myth 1: “It’s a peptide, so it’s natural and safe.”

Your body makes peptides. That does not make every synthetic research compound a wellness gummy.

## Myth 2: “RUO means advanced users only.”

RUO is an intended-use boundary, not a rank insignia.

## Myth 3: “99% purity means it works.”

Purity is about what’s in the vial chemically — not whether the claim is clinically true.

## Myth 4: “If a clinic offers it, it’s approved.”

Availability ≠ approval for that use. Ask what evidence and what regulatory pathway they’re operating under.

## Myth 5: “Anecdotes from big guys beat papers.”

Big guys can also be wrong, enhanced with ten other variables, or selling a stack. Papers aren’t perfect — but “trust me bro” isn’t a method.
