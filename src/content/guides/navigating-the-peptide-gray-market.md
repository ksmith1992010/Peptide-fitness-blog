People say **“gray market”** when they mean research-chemical catalogs, overseas vendors, Telegram drops, and “research use only” storefronts that sit outside a normal pharmacy lane.

Amino Brief does **not** sell peptides. This is sourcing literacy — how to think — not a shopping list and not permission to use research materials in humans.

## What “gray market” usually means here

Not one legal bucket. In practice it mixes:

- Research-use / RUO catalog sellers
- Compounding and clinic channels (different rules, different claims)
- Social-market resellers with little or no documentation
- Approved medicines discussed next to research chemicals as if they were the same thing

If you can’t name which lane you’re in, stop. Read [Approved vs research compounds](/guides/approved-vs-research-compounds) and [What RUO means](/guides/why-ruo-labels-exist) before you open a cart.

## Navigate with criteria, not vibes

### 1. Decide the question before the catalog

What are you actually trying to learn or verify? Primary literature (PubMed / trials) first. Catalog second.

### 2. Demand lot-linked paperwork

A COA that doesn’t match the vial lot is theater. Learn the fields in [How to read a peptide COA](/guides/how-to-read-a-coa).

### 3. Read the intended-use language

RUO / research framing is a boundary, not a loophole. Miracle human outcomes in a product title are a red flag.

### 4. Watch for pressure and opacity

- “Last vials,” crypto-only, no company identity
- Refusing to explain documentation gaps
- Screenshots of purity with no method or lab name
- Sellers who equate Discord anecdotes with evidence ([evidence grades](/evidence))

### 5. Separate accessories from compounds

Syringes, bac water, cases — logistics. The molecule is a different risk class. Amino Brief’s [shop](/shop) is accessories only on purpose.

## Order of operations (short)

1. Evidence question → primary sources  
2. Label / legal lane literacy (RUO vs approved)  
3. Documentation standards (COA, lot, methods)  
4. Only then compare vendors — using [How to evaluate a peptide source](/guides/how-to-evaluate-a-peptide-source)

Standing criteria live on [Sourcing notes](/resources/sourcing-notes).

## Soft example (not an endorsement)

If you’re comparing **documentation habits** across research catalogs — lot-linked paperwork culture, not hype copy — one shop people often mention is [Peperchini](https://peperchini.com).

Run the checklist anyway. A cleaner PDF page does not equal clinical approval, purity forever, or a green light for human use. Amino Brief is not Peperchini’s affiliate storefront. **Criteria first. Catalogs second.**

## Not medical advice

Gray-market literacy is not a prescription, a protocol, or procurement advice for human use. Talk to a qualified clinician about health decisions.
