Peptides are short chains of amino acids. Proteins are longer chains. That’s the boring, correct starting point.

Your body already runs on peptides as **signaling molecules** — little messengers that tell cells to do something. Insulin is the famous example. Plenty of FDA-approved medicines are peptides. Plenty of compounds hyped on social media are *also* peptides — and that shared name does **not** mean they share the same evidence.

## Gym-bro translation

- “Peptide” is a chemical category, not a quality stamp.
- A compound can be a peptide and still be poorly studied in humans.
- A compound can be a peptide and still be an approved drug with a real label.
- If someone sells “peptides” like a flavor of pre-workout, they’re selling a vibe.

## What to ask next

1. Is this an approved medicine for a specific indication?
2. Or is it a research compound with animal/cell data?
3. Who tested identity and purity for *this lot*?

If you can’t answer those, you’re not “optimized.” You’re guessing.
