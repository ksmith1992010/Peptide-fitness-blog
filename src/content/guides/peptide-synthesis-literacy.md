The newsroom keeps surfacing **how peptides are made** — solid-phase tricks for hindered sequences, electrochemical modification, even prebiotic formation chemistry.

Useful if you care whether a vial’s story is chemically plausible. Useless as a “how I cook peptides in my garage” guide (we will not go there).

## Takeaways for Amino Brief readers

- **Synthesis route ≠ clinical grade.** A clever method paper does not certify a catalog lot.
- **Hard sequences are hard for a reason.** If a vendor claims exotic constructs with zero method language, raise an eyebrow.
- **Prebiotic / origins-of-life papers** are curiosity science — not wellness product origin myths.
- **Quality still collapses to documentation** — identity, purity method, lot match. See [How to read a COA](/guides/how-to-read-a-coa).

## Desk rule

When a synthesis paper lands in `/desk`, we ask: does this change how a lay reader evaluates claims or catalogs? If yes → literacy brief. If it’s only for practicing chemists → leave it as a draft lead.
