Cone-snail venom peptides (conotoxins) show up in structural papers on **antinociceptive mechanisms** and transporter biology. Fascinating. Extremely easy to misuse in content.

## Hard boundaries for Amino Brief

- We will describe the **research lane** (structure, target, model systems).
- We will **not** translate conotoxin work into human analgesic protocols.
- Pain management is clinician territory. Venom-derived peptides are not “natural Advil.”

## How to read the headline without getting wrecked

1. **Antinociceptive in a paper** ≠ approved pain drug.
2. **Transporter mechanism** ≠ safe research-catalog shopping list.
3. If a seller uses a conotoxin paper to hype a mystery vial, run the [sourcing checklist](/guides/how-to-evaluate-a-peptide-source) and walk away if docs are theater.
