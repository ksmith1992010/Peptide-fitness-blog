G protein–coupled receptors (GPCRs) are a huge drug-target class. Many endogenous ligands are peptides. Desk drafts on **ligand discovery**, **biased agonism**, and **neurokinin receptor selectivity** belong in the “serious pharmacology” bucket.

## Literacy translation

- **Ligand discovery** = finding / designing molecules that bind a receptor in research systems.
- **Biased agonist** = prefers some signaling pathways over others in those systems — not a marketing adjective for a research SKU.
- **Selectivity work** (e.g. NK2R) is structure–function science — not a pain or gut “hack.”

## How this connects to Amino Brief compounds

When forums rename everything as a “GPCR peptide,” push back with evidence grades. For FDA-context incretin / growth-hormone-axis medicines, start at the labeled product and [evidence tiers](/guides/evidence-tiers-cell-to-human) — not a methods review.
