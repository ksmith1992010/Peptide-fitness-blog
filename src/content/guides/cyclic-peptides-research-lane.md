Desk pull: papers on **membrane-permeable cyclic peptides** that inhibit protein–protein interactions. That is medicinal-chemistry / chemical-biology territory — not a Telegram “cyclic stack.”

## What “cyclic peptide” means in literacy terms

A peptide whose backbone (or side chains) closes into a ring. Cyclization can change protease stability and cell entry behavior in research systems. It does **not** mean a consumer product is validated, approved, or safe for DIY use.

## How to read a cyclic-peptide methods paper

1. **Assay first** — cell, biochemical, animal, or chemistry-only?
2. **Target clarity** — which PPI / pathway, measured how?
3. **Delivery claims** — “membrane permeable” in a model ≠ “take this at home.”
4. **Intended-use gap** — journal methods ≠ research-catalog SKU ≠ medicine.

## Where people self-own

- Treating a Nature methods title as a buying brief
- Confusing constrained peptides for imaging or oncology research with gym recovery lore
- Skipping identity / lot docs because the paper “proved permeability”
